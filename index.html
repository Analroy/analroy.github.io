About

I am a Ph.D. student at the School of Mathematical & Computational Sciences (SMCS), Indian Association for the Cultivation of Science (IACS), Kolkata, under the supervision of Dr. Debarshi Kumar Sanyal. My current research focuses on multimodal learning and Indian Sign Language (ISL) processing — developing vision–language and motion-aware deep learning systems to bridge the communication gap for the deaf and hard-of-hearing community in India.

Prior to my Ph.D., I worked as a Project Linked Personnel at ISI Kolkata (2021–2023) under Dr. Swagatam Das, where I investigated multiple kernel methods for multi-view clustering and co-authored a comprehensive survey published in Information Fusion (Elsevier, 2025). I also hold an Indian Patent (IN580844 B1) for an agonist-free platelet function detection system using UV light and deep learning.

I hold an M.Sc. in Electronic Science from Acharya Prafulla Chandra College (WBSU) and a B.Sc. in Physics (Hons.) from the University of Calcutta.
