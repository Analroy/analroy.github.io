Agonist-free system and method for detecting platelet function by stimulation of platelet aggregation
Application No.: 201931048635  |  Filing Date: 27 November 2019  |  Publication Date: 17 December 2021
Applicant: Sangutech Enterprises Private Limited (incubated at CRNN, University of Calcutta)
International Classification: G06N3/08, G06N3/04, G06K9/62, G01N33/86, G06K9/00
Inventors:
Suryyani Deb · Ranjini Chowdhury · Abhishek Sadhu · Anal Roy Chowdhury · Anjan Kumar Dasgupta · Ashish Kumar Dhara · Krishnendu Roy · Maitreyee Bhattacharyya · Amlan Chakrabarti
Abstract:
The invention provides an agonist-free system and method for detecting platelet function using UV light as a stimulant for platelet aggregation. Microscopic images of UV-treated platelet samples are analysed using a Multi-scale Fully Convolutional Network (MFCN) combined with a trained deep neural network, enabling accurate and reagent-free point-of-care assessment of platelet function.

Background & Significance

This invention emerged from a DBT-BIRAC (Government of India) funded R&D project on agonist-free point-of-care platelet function testing. Conventional platelet aggregation assays require chemical agonists (e.g., ADP, collagen), which introduce reagent costs and handling complexity. Our system uses UV light as a non-chemical stimulant and employs deep learning image analysis to quantify platelet aggregation from microscopic images — enabling fast, low-cost, reagent-free platelet function assessment with potential applications in cardiovascular diagnostics and pre-operative screening.

The work was carried out at CRNN, University of Calcutta, and involved collaboration with Dr. Ashis Kumar Dhara at NIT Durgapur for the deep learning image analysis pipeline.
